The 10 Mistakes

1
Trading Without a Stop Loss

This is the #1 account killer. Many beginners think "it will come back" or "I'll exit manually when needed." This thinking has destroyed more portfolios than any market crash.

Without a stop loss, a single bad trade can wipe out weeks or months of gains. Professional traders ALWAYS use stop losses ‚Äî no exceptions.

‚úÖ Solution: Set your stop loss BEFORE entering any trade. A good rule: never risk more than 1-2% of your total capital on a single trade. If you have $1,000, your maximum loss per trade should be $10-20.
2
Using Too Much Leverage

Exchanges offer 100x, even 125x leverage. Beginners see this as "easy money" ‚Äî turn $100 into $10,000! In reality, high leverage means a 1% move against you can liquidate your entire position.

With 100x leverage, a 1% price drop = 100% loss. Your position is gone.

‚úÖ Solution: Stick to 2x-5x leverage maximum as a beginner. Many successful traders use 2x-3x or even no leverage at all. Lower leverage = more room for error = longer survival in the market.

3
FOMO Trading (Fear of Missing Out)

You see a coin pumping 50%. Twitter is going crazy. Your friend just made $5,000. You buy at the top... and watch it crash 40% the next day.

FOMO is one of the most dangerous emotions in trading. By the time something is "all over social media," the smart money has already taken profits.

‚úÖ Solution: Never chase green candles. If you missed a move, let it go ‚Äî there will always be another opportunity. Wait for pullbacks to support levels. The best entries come when others are fearful, not euphoric.
4
No Trading Plan

"I'll just see how it goes" is not a strategy. Trading without a plan means making decisions based on emotions rather than logic. This leads to inconsistent results and eventual losses.

‚úÖ Solution: Before EVERY trade, write down:
  • Entry price: Where exactly will you buy?
  • Stop loss: Where will you exit if wrong?
  • Take profit: Where will you secure gains?
  • Position size: How much will you risk?
  • Reason: Why are you taking this trade?

5
Overtrading

More trades ‚â† more profits. In fact, the opposite is usually true. Each trade costs you fees and exposes you to risk. Trading out of boredom or the need for "action" is a recipe for losses.

Professional traders often take only 2-5 quality trades per week. Some take even fewer. Quality over quantity.

‚úÖ Solution: Set a maximum number of trades per day/week. If you've hit your limit, stop trading. Wait for high-conviction setups only. It's better to miss opportunities than to force bad trades.
6
Moving Your Stop Loss

You set a stop loss at $95. Price drops to $96. You think "it's so close, let me move it to $90 to give it more room." Price drops to $91. You move it to $85. Eventually you're down 30% on a trade that should have been a 5% loss.

‚úÖ Solution: NEVER move your stop loss further away from your entry. The only acceptable move is to bring it closer (to lock in profits as the trade goes your way). Your original analysis was correct ‚Äî trust it.

7
Revenge Trading

You just lost $200. You're angry. You NEED to make it back. So you immediately take another trade ‚Äî bigger this time ‚Äî without proper analysis. You lose another $300. Now you're furious...

This emotional spiral is called "revenge trading" and it's responsible for more blown accounts than almost anything else.

‚úÖ Solution: After any loss, take a mandatory break. Walk away from the screen for at least 1-2 hours. Journal about what went wrong. Never trade angry. Set a daily loss limit (e.g., 3% of capital) ‚Äî if you hit it, you're done for the day.
8
Ignoring Risk/Reward Ratio

If your stop loss is $50 away but your take profit is only $25 away, you're risking $2 to make $1. Even with a 60% win rate, you'll lose money over time.

Risk/Reward Win Rate Needed to Break Even
1:1 50%
1:2 33%
1:3 25%
‚úÖ Solution: Only take trades with at least 1:2 risk/reward. This means your potential profit should be at least 2x your potential loss. With 1:3 R/R, you can be wrong 70% of the time and still be profitable!

9
Not Taking Profits

Your trade is up 50%. You think "this is going to 100%!" You hold. It pulls back to 30%. You think "it'll go back up." It drops to 10%. You're now hoping to just break even on a trade that was up 50%.

Greed kills profits. Paper gains mean nothing until you realize them.

‚úÖ Solution: Use scaled take profits. Example:
  • Take 25% profit at TP1
  • Take 25% profit at TP2
  • Take 25% profit at TP3
  • Let 25% ride with trailing stop
This locks in gains while still capturing upside.
10
Trading Money You Can't Afford to Lose

Using rent money, emergency funds, or borrowed money to trade creates unbearable pressure. This pressure leads to emotional decisions, which lead to losses, which create more pressure. It's a death spiral.

‚úÖ Solution: Only trade with money that, if lost completely, would not affect your life. This isn't just financial advice ‚Äî it's psychological. When you trade with "scared money," you make scared decisions.
